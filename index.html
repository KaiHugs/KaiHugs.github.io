Introduction

Hey everyone!

My name is Kai Hughes and I would love to represent our program year within MES. I have a passion for mentorship, community-development, and physics T-shirts. Alongside this passion, I have strong experience amongst executive teams and organizing group-run events. I strive to make the best experience for our program through positivity and open-minded innovation.

I have also recently developed an obssession for Ping Pong...

The foremost reason I want to represent my program this year in McMaster Engineering Society is because I want to be the voice of change and community feedback. Throughout my high school career, I always sought out leadership roles; tutoring, positions on executive teams, sports, or simply offering another mind to everyday problems. As a result, being able to represent a program's year while making a change that would benefit students and the engineering community as a whole was enticing to me. I have many ideas I would love to share with the institution and if you have any questions or suggestions - please feel free to contact me!

Thank you so much!

Mental Health for Engineers

Undoubtedly, engineering is one of the most mentally demanding and challenging majors in university. Thus, this can contribute to a unique set of mental health pressure from yourself, your classmates, parents, professors, etc... It is not abnormal to feel at such high stakes where perfectionism is matched with tight deadlines; this can feel mentally exhausting and bestow feelings of inadequacy or self-doubt. This only briefly acknowledges the aspects of a school setting. Not mentioning the isolation and independence one might feel in a social setting which ruins a support structure from other humans. It is hard. It is hard to find a proper work-life balance while managing your own stress, fatigue, and academics.

Pedagogical Innovation

Aspiring engineers have not had a traditional style of learning and thus many foundational concepts are not refined in a manner that would allow a continuation of traditional style teaching. Especially working straight to interactive hands-on applications, it is inherently more difficult for students that have not had even 2 years of practical experience in this regard. Virtual labs and simulations, although are good resources for practice, are no way of bridging the gap of true material learning.
